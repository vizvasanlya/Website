Is Rummy legal in India?

Yes, online Rummy is legal in India. In 1968, the Supreme Court of India passed a judgment that deemed rummy a game of skill. However, a few states, including Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Nagaland, and Sikkim, have restrictions on skill-based games involving stakes.

How do I form valid sequences and sets in Rummy?

In Rummy, a sequence is a group of three or more consecutive cards of the same suit, while a set is a group of three or four cards of the same rank but different suits. To win, you need at least one pure sequence (without any Joker) and one other sequence. The remaining cards can be arranged in valid sequences or sets.

How is the score calculated in Points Rummy?

In Points Rummy, the score is calculated based on the ungrouped cards of the losing players. The winner's score is the sum of the points of the ungrouped cards of all opponents, multiplied by the point value of the game.
